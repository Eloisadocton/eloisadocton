Designer. Illustrator. Developer


For some people, UI Design, design thinking, empathy and user centered design are new concepts. To me though, UI design is simply common sense design, I live and breathe designing for real people, I love narrating customer journeys, discovering problems and coming up with creative solutions - it's what I do.
During my college years I worked in the service industry, hotels, conventions, retail, you name it. During that time, I learned that if customers aren't right, they are unhappy, and, truthfully, if a customer is not happy, right or not, who will pay the consequences?
When I started illustrating, doing good research like who my client was, who they catered, how they used my designs, what time of the year they needed the designs for, the demographic of their customers, any limitations and so on, were all things that dictated how successful the project would be. Investing the time to learn about who the design was for and what solutions the design would serve were key to making design decissions.
When I moved onto web design, the same fundamentals worked best, research, create, then improve by testing. Because I'm also a numbers person and have analytics and demand backgrounds, I often use those skills with user research because it allows me to quantify how successful an implementation has been.
